Lasagna

Description

Lasagna is a popular Italian baked pasta dish consisting of alternating layers of pasta sheets, sauce, cheese, and often meat or vegetables. Here are the key aspects of lasagna:

Lasagna originated in Italy during the Middle Ages. The oldest known written recipe dates back to the 14th century, though earlier versions likely existed. The dish has evolved over time, with tomatoes being introduced after the 16th century when they arrived in Europe from the Americas.

Ingredients

  • Flat, wide pasta noodles (lasagna sheets)
  • Sauce (usually tomato-based meat sauce or ragù)
  • Cheese (commonly ricotta, mozzarella, and Parmesan)
  • Optional meat (ground beef, Italian sausage) or vegetables

Steps

  1. Prepare the meat sauce
  2. Make the cheese mixture
  3. Cook the lasagna noodles
  4. Preheat the oven to 350°F
  5. Assemble the lasagna layers
  6. Cover with foil and bake
  7. Uncover and continue baking
  8. Let it rest
  9. Serve and enjoy
